# Collection / Exploration Reference

Use this for coin collectors, scavenger hunts, traversal-focused exploration, light objective games, and chill discovery loops.

## What Must Be Clear in the Spec

- what the player is collecting, finding, or activating
- why traversal is satisfying
- how the world guides the player route
- whether the game is timed, score-based, objective-based, or open-ended
- what creates escalation: denser routes, trickier placement, hazards, time pressure

## Good Clarifying Questions

- Is the fun mainly movement, route planning, discovery, or optimization?
- Is the player trying to find everything or just enough to finish?
- Are there hazards, or is this mostly peaceful?
- Is the map hand-authored, semi-random, or endless?
- What makes repeat play interesting: faster time, better score, new layouts?

## Scope Traps

- huge maps with little interaction density
- too many collectible types with weak meaning
- progression systems before the route is fun
- decorative worldbuilding that does not support navigation

## Healthy V1 Shape

- one compact space with readable landmarks
- one collectible type or one primary objective
- one clear success condition
- optional timer, score, or bonus route pressure

## Strong Early Slices

1. Traversal and readable space layout
2. Collectible placement and pickup feedback
3. Objective tracking / score / timer
4. End-state and replay loop
5. Optional hazards or route mastery twists

## Signs the Spec Is Too Thin

- the player goal is just "collect stuff"
- there is no reason to choose one route over another
- collectibles do not affect pacing or decisions
- the game has atmosphere but no progression through a run
